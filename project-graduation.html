Project Graduation FAQ

What‚Äôs the Backstory?

Project Graduation (often called ‚ÄúProject Grad‚Äù) began at Oxford Hills High School in Maine in 1980 following a tragic car accident. The program was created to provide graduating seniors with a fun, memorable, and safe graduation-night experience on a night that has historically been associated with poor decision-making.

How Is It Organized?

At KHS, Project Graduation is organized by a small group of parent volunteers along with the Class Advisor(s). All parents are welcome to join the planning committee‚Äîwe typically begin meeting in late spring.

Together, we plan an inclusive and exciting celebration designed to appeal to the entire graduating class. The details of the event are kept a surprise until students board the buses after graduation, which adds to the excitement.

Past experiences have included:

  • Harbor cruises
  • Cranmore Outdoor Adventure Park
  • Go-karts and arcades
  • Hypnotist shows and comedians
  • Laser tag
  • Tons of food: dinner, midnight snacks, drinks, and breakfast for over 150 teens and volunteers!

The event usually begins around 5:00 PM and concludes with our return to KHS at approximately 6:00 AM the following morning.

Who Can Participate?

The event is open to all KHS graduating seniors, and we truly hope every student will attend.

What If My Child Is Unsure?

Encourage them to talk with their friends, counselor, or a teacher to get a sense of whether this is something they‚Äôd enjoy. While it is a long night, KHS typically sees about 90% class participation, which speaks to how positive and meaningful the experience is for most students.

How Much Does It Cost?

The average cost is approximately $225 per student.

This reflects the reality that not every venue can accommodate 150 students at once, and many of the places we use remain open after normal business hours, which increases costs.

Why Do You Need Funds Now?

To secure the best venues and pricing‚Äîand avoid being shut out of desirable options‚Äîwe need to book locations in the summer/early fall. Reserving these spaces requires large deposits for items such as buses, venues, and meals.

It‚Äôs important to remember that nearly every high school in New England hosts a Grad Night, so we are competing with many other schools to lock in venues. Waiting until the spring before graduation typically means fewer options and potentially higher costs.

Why Not Keep It Simple (e.g., a Bonfire on the Beach)?

Liability and safety considerations make this unrealistic. Even a casual event still requires transportation, food, insurance, supervision, and secured locations. Project Graduation‚Äôs structure ensures a controlled, safe environment throughout the night.
